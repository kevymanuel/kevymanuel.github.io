To grow subscription revenue and improve the experience for gift-oriented users, I led the development and launch of the Tinybeans Gift Card product. This solution allowed users to easily gift a premium subscription, solved a recurring support issue, and opened new promotional and B2B distribution opportunities through partnerships and Tinybeans+ Business.

üîç Problem Statement

During user research for a referral initiative, we discovered:

  • 65%+ of users joined Tinybeans after seeing it via a friend or family member.
  • Users frequently requested the ability to gift subscriptions, especially during holidays or life milestones like new births.
  • 3% of premium subscribers were not the account's primary users (e.g., grandparents purchasing on behalf of parents), resulting in workaround flows that negatively affected user experience and data integrity.

This insight pointed to an untapped opportunity to launch a new product that could solve gifting friction while driving revenue growth.

üí° Solution

Develop a Gift Card product to:

  • Enable seamless gifting of Tinybeans+ subscriptions
  • Eliminate the need for manual workarounds
  • Lay the foundation for commercial and promotional partnerships with hospitals, baby registries, and employers

üîß Strategy & Execution

üéØ Use Case Development

  • Consumer (MVP): Users gift subscriptions directly via Tinybeans.com
  • Commercial (Future): Businesses like hospitals or employers gift Tinybeans to new parents or employees
  • Promotional (Future): Distributed through platforms like Babylist

üõ† Technical & Product Approach

  • Purchase/redemption experience hosted on Tinybeans.com for faster iteration and full control of UX and communications
  • Integrated Stripe Checkout for lower fees, flexible payment options, and data control
  • Backend designed with scalability in mind to support future commercial/promotional use cases

üí¨ Subscription Design

  • Introduced 6-month and 12-month durations (no monthly)
  • Non-renewing gift cards to avoid billing ambiguity
  • Designed a renewal messaging flow to help convert gifted users into paying subscribers post-gift

üì≤ User Flow Overview

‚úÖ Purchase Flow

  1. User taps "Gift Card" in the app menu
  2. Chooses "Purchase" option
  3. Redirected to Tinybeans.com to:
    • Select duration (6 or 12 months)
    • Enter recipient info, sender name, optional message
    • Choose send date
    • Checkout via Stripe (supports Apple Pay, Google Pay, Klarna, etc.)

üéÅ Redemption Flow

  1. Recipient receives an email with gift card code and redemption link
  2. In the app, they tap "Redeem", enter the code, and activate Tinybeans+
